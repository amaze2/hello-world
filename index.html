About CV Portfolio

I am a M.S. candidate in Library and Information Science at Simmons University (May 2019), where I specialize in digital technology. I also earned a Ph.D. in English Language and Literature in 2014 from the University of Michigan, where I specialized in Victorian poetry. My goal is to combine these two fields and serve as an academic librarian in the digital humanities, scholarly communication, or research & instruction / first-year experience.

My work experience is similarly panoramic. I have over 15 years of experience in higher education and cultural heritage institutions, including facilitating digital humanities initiatives for Northeastern University Libraries' Digital Scholarship Group, processing collections for Boston City Archives, providing reference and circulation support for the Wheelock College Library and U-M's Clark Library, and teaching English literature and academic writing at the University of Michigan.

I am also a published author on topics ranging from Victorian poetry, to the digital humanities, to the archival profession. My peer-reviewed articles have appeared in Victorian Literature and Culture, Victorian Poetry, and Nineteenth-Century Literature, and I write the annual "Guide to the Year's Work" on Algernon Charles Swinburne for Victorian Poetry.

In my free time, I enjoy running. I have completed nine marathons, including New York, Chicago, and Boston, with a personal best of 3:02 (2012). My wife is the remarkable Liz Kaziunas.
